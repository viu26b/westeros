Old Valyria

Old Valyria, located on the eastern continent of Essos, was once considered the greatest city in the known world.
Containing 14 huge, magic-infused, volcanic mountains known as the 14 flames,
it was a city of wonderment and was once the capital of a great empire, the Valyrian Freehold.
On this peninsula, its people, Valyrians, learned how to train, tame, and ride dragons.
This was unheard of in other lands and made Valyrians feel otherworldly to the point where most even believed that they were descended from the beasts.
Though Valyria had no kings, the city was ruled by wealthy Dragonlord families who practiced blood magic and other dark arts.
During this time, House Targaryen, one of the noble Dragonlord families, was not considered especially powerful.
This changed however, after the Doom of Valyria. 12 years prior to the Doom, Lord Aenar Targaryen's daughter, Daenys the Dreamer, had a prophetic dream and visions that depicted Varlyria's destruction.
Believing the prophecy, Aenar took his family and their five dragons and moved them out of Valyria and into Dragonstone, an island on the outskirts of Westeros.

Doom of Valyria

12 years after House Targaryen fled Valyria,
some unknown catalyst caused the fires of the 14 flames to erupt on the Valyrian freehold,
destroying the city in a matter of hours. Every hill for 500 miles split,
filling the air with ash, smoke, and fire. All in its path was consumed, even the dragons.
The earth opened up and swallowed entire towns, temples, and palaces.
The lakes of Valyria turned to acid and fiery fountains shot molten rock thousands of feet into the air.
A portion of northern Valyria collapsed into the sea and with Valyria's destruction,
almost all of the dragon rider families were destroyed as well.
The events of the Doom made House Targaryen the most powerful family
of dragonlords as they were now the only ones left.

House Targaryen

House Targaryen is a noble family of Valyrian descent.
As the only family of dragonlords to survive the Doom of Valyria, the Targaryens spent over a century residing on the island of Dragonstone in a castle of the same name.
This was until Aegon Targaryen I also known as Aegon the Conqueror set out with his sisters, older sister, Visenya, and younger sister, Rhaenys, and their dragons to take over Westeros.
The siblings together with their dragons: Vhagar, Meraxes, and Balerion the Dread, began the first of the Wars of Conquest. The result of these wars would lead to the Targaryen rule over Westeros, which spanned over 3 centuries.
The House sigil has a black background and a three-headed red dragon breathing flames on the front of it.
The House words are "Fire and Blood" as the use of dragon fire and spilling the blood of their enemies, is how the Targaryens go about their takeover.

Westeros

By the time the Targaryens set their sights on taking over Westeros, the region was already used to invasion. Westeros was first invaded, inhabited, and populated by the First Men, and then again by the Andals. Seven Kingdoms had formed under seven noble houses, each ruling its own region (and occasionally warring with each other over boundaries and land).

Wars of Conquest

House Targaryen seemed content to live out the rest of its existence as the rulers of Dragonstone and nothing more, until Aegon Targaryen I was born. Young, strong, bold, assertive, and arrogant, Aegon had a taste for battle and aspirations that stretched much farther than Dragonstone. With the mindset of re-establishing the great Valyrian empire, Aegon set his sights west toward Westeros. He began his conquest initially by holding a meeting with his sisters and his bannermen, wherein he wrote and sent letters to every ruler in the Seven Kingdoms announcing himself as the new king of a unified realm. He promised that those who submitted to him as king of the realm would keep their lands and titles, and that those who didn't would be destroyed. Some ruling Houses submitted and kneeled immediately while others chose to stand their ground and wage war. In response to this, Aegon separated his forces into 3 teams, with each team consisting of members of the Targaryen army and the armies of their bannermen, as well as escorted by each Targaryen heir and their dragon. These are the Wars of Conquest.

War in the Stormlands
In the Stormlands, Aegon and his sisters were up against the King of the Stormlands, King Argilac, and his army. With an army that far outnumbered that of the Targaryens, King Argilac felt certain of victory. However, the reality of his enemies' dragonfire soon laid waste to most of King Argilac's army, and he lost his life while in a one-on-one duel against the enemy. Now with control over the Stormlands, Aegon appointed one his bannermen, Orys Baratheon, the new ruler of the Stormlands and House Baratheon as the new noble house of those lands.

War in the Riverlands
In the Riverlands, Aegon the Conqueror was now up against the King of both the Iron Islands and the Riverlands, King Harren of House Hoare. Aided by the Targaryen army, his dragon, and a popular uprising against the ironborn king led by House Tully (of the Riverlands), Aegon successfully decimated the enemy army and holed King Harren in his castle called Harrenhal. Even with no place to turn, King Harren still refused to surrender and submit, so Aegon had his dragon, Balerion the Dread, burn Harrenhal to the ground with King Harren, his family, and his remaining supporters still in it.

War in the Westernlands
In the Westernlands, Aegon and his legion saw their most difficult battle yet. With the combined forces of House Lannister and House Gardener, Aegon was up against over 55,000 soldiers ready for battle. The numbers were so far from his favor that Aegon and his sisters released all 3 of their dragons on the enemy. The aftermath saw the full destruction of House Gardener and an eventual submission of House Lannister. House Lannister was now made Lords of the Westernlands while House Tyrell (former stewards of House Gardener) was made Lords of the Reach.

War in the North & the Vale
House Stark of the North and House Arryn of the Vale mobilized troops and prepared for battle. However, upon hearing of Aegon's dragons and the conquest in other lands, King Torrhen Stark of the North decided to bend the knee to save his people and his army from certain destruction. In the Vale, boy-king Ronnel Arryn, also submitted peacefully in exchange for a ride on one of the dragons. House Stark was made Lords of the North and House Arryn was made Lords of the Vale. With the conquering of the Vale, Aegon had successfully unified six of the seven kingdoms under his rule.

King's Landing: The Red Keep

After successfully conquering six of the seven kingdoms and taking his new position as the overall ruler and King of Westeros, Aegon had the Red Keep built. The Red Keep is a castle that was made using bright red bricks. It was rumored that Aegon chose the color for the bricks specifically so that his Westerosi subjects wouldn't forget the dragon fire he roasted his enemies with. A visual reminder of the consequence of defiance. The Red Keep is also where Aegon collected all the swords of his enemies and had his dragon, Balerion the Dread, melt them into his Iron Throne.

Religion: Faith of the Seven

Though there are a few varying beliefs in Westeros, the most observed religion is the Faith of the Seven.
The Faith of the Seven holds that there is one god with seven different aspects, each symbolizing a different area of life.
They are: the Mother, the Father, the Warrior, the Maiden, the Crone, the Smith, the Stranger.

The Stranger is the aspect least prayed to. It represents death and the unknown.

Believers of the Faith, pray to the Mother for grace and for mercy. She watches over fertility, childbirth, and peace.

Believers of the Faith, pray to the Father for divine justice as he watches over and judges the souls of the dead.

Believers of the Faith, pray to the Warrior for protection, valor, and skill in battle. He represents strength and courage.

Believers of the Faith, pray to the Maiden for purity, love, innocence, and beauty


Believers of the Faith, pray to the Smith for creation and craftsmanship.

Believers of the Faith, pray to the Crone for wisdom and foresight.

Valyrian Steel

Valyrian steel is a metal form that was unearthed in Old Valyria during the days of the powerful Valyrian Freehold. When used to make bladed weaponry,
Valyrian steel was praised for its strength, durability, light-weight, and sharpness.
Weapons made from Valyrian steel also never dulled so once created,
a weapon forged from the steel could last generations and remain untarnished.
After the Doom of Valyria, no new Valyrian steel weapons could be made, as the city's destruction vanished the metal. The Valyrian steel weapons owned by the Targaryens and the ones circulating via trade are the only ones that remain,
so they are considered both rare and very valuable.
